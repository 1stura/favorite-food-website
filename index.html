Chicken Katsu

Crispy, Tender, and Delicious

Discover the mouthwatering flavors of this Japanese classic.

What is Chicken Katsu?

Chicken katsu is a beloved Japanese dish featuring a crispy, deep-fried chicken cutlet coated in panko breadcrumbs. It's typically served with a savory tonkatsu sauce, shredded cabbage, and rice.
This flavorful dish is known for its satisfying crunch, tender meat, and irresistible combination of textures and flavors.
It was originally invented in Tokyo during the late 1800's by an unnamed restaurant who wanted to replicate european style meat. Perhaps that's why the dish is so popular among Europeans and Americans.

Wanna make it?

Key Ingredients

  • Boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Eggs
  • Flour
  • Oil for deep-frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Shredded cabbage
  • Cooked rice

How to Make Chicken Katsu

  1. Pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness.
  2. Season the chicken with salt and pepper.
  3. Dredge the chicken in flour, then dip in beaten egg, and finally coat with panko breadcrumbs.
  4. Deep-fry the chicken in hot oil until golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Slice the chicken into cutlets.
  6. Serve with tonkatsu sauce, shredded cabbage, and rice.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Katsu

  • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients.
  • Pound the chicken evenly for consistent cooking.
  • Don't overcrowd the pan when frying.
  • Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  • Serve immediately for optimal crispness.

Conclusion:

Overall chicken katsu is a wonderful alternative to traditional unhealthy battered and fried chicken.
